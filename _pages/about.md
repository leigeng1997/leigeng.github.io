I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the group of [Prof. Philipp Werner](https://www.unifr.ch/phys/en/research/groups/werner/) at the University of Fribourg, Switzerland. My current research focuses on nonequilibrium dynamics in strongly correlated systems, with particular emphasis on long-lived transient (prethermal-like) states under photodoping and constant electric fields. To study these problems, I mainly use nonequilibrium Green's functions and nonequilibrium dynamical mean-field theory (DMFT).

During my PhD at Peking University, I worked in the group of [Prof. Liang-You Peng](https://faculty.pku.edu.cn/pengliangyou/zh_CN/index/9858/list/index.htm) on strong-field ionization and high-harmonic generation in atoms and molecules. As an undergraduate, I also received early research training in atmospheric chemistry in the group of [Prof. Tzung-May Fu](https://atmoschem.org.cn/index.php?title=Tzung-May_FU) at Peking University.

I am broadly interested in many areas of physics, especially computational and numerical approaches to complex problems. If you are interested in my previous work or would like to discuss related ideas, feel free to contact me.
